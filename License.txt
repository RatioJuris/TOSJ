Protection under this licence attaches provisionally and immediately upon publication of an Article bearing the notice set out in How to Apply These Terms. The CryptoID is a standard cryptographic hash of the author's file, computed either by the author directly or by The Open Source Journal's portal upon the author's upload; both methods use the same hashing standard and produce an identical fingerprint for an identical file, so the Article's protection under this paragraph does not depend on which method the author used. Generating a CryptoID requires no review, no approval, and no waiting period for this provisional protection to take effect.

The CryptoID is proof of submission, not a certificate or proof of originality. Its uniqueness proves only that the submitted file matches that fingerprint. It does not, and cannot, certify that the Article's content is original. An exact copy of an existing work produces the same hash as the original and is caught immediately as a duplicate; a paraphrased, translated, or otherwise reworded copy produces a different hash while remaining just as plagiarised, and a hash alone cannot detect it. The OSJOID is issued only once evaluated, and maps a given author, project, and CryptoID value to one another; it is what converts the provisional protection above into a confirmed, verified claim of authorship.
