# CHARTER OF THE OPEN SOURCE JOURNAL

* **Published:** 19 July 2026
* **Promulgated by:** Ronav Isrgrajan Narayandas (also known as Ratio Juris), on behalf of The Open Source Journal

---

*This Charter organises, in Articles and Chapters, the governance structure already established by the [OSJ Doctrine](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md). It restates the Doctrine's institutions in a familiar constitutional form, in the tradition of the United Nations Charter,<sup id="cfnref1"><a class="fnref" href="#cfn1">[1]</a></sup> adapted throughout to the purposes of The Open Source Journal. It creates no right and removes no protection that the Doctrine does not itself create or remove. Under [Section 2(i)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) and [Section 4(4)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#4-the-soul-and-the-body) of the Doctrine, this Charter belongs to the Body. Where any Article of this Charter conflicts with the Doctrine, the Doctrine prevails.*

## PREAMBLE

WE, the independent scholars, developers, Nodes, and Members of The Open Source Journal,

DETERMINED to save intellectual advancement from the artificial scarcity of institutional gatekeeping, and to affirm the right of every human being to share, learn, and elevate the human condition without pedigree, permission, or payment,

RECOGNISING that trust in a Manuscript must be earned through proof-of-execution, open scrutiny, and peer replication, and never purchased through credential, subscription, or certificate,

RESOLVED to combine our effort to eradicate Gatekeeping in all its forms, academic and technological alike, and to establish institutions sufficient to maintain this framework without themselves becoming the very gatekeepers this Charter exists to abolish,

HAVE AGREED to this Charter of The Open Source Journal, and do hereby establish the organs and procedures set out below.

## CHAPTER I: PURPOSES, PRINCIPLES, AND DEFINITIONS (ARTICLES 1-3)

### Article 1

The purposes of The Open Source Journal are:

1. To secure the free transmission of knowledge, and to bring about, by peaceful and open means, the abolition of Gatekeeping as defined in the Doctrine;
2. To develop cooperation among independent creators, Nodes, and co-operating Nation-States in the advancement of science, technology, and human understanding;
3. To be a centre for harmonising the actions of Members in furtherance of these common ends, without itself becoming a credentialing or gatekeeping authority.

### Article 2

The Open Source Journal and its Members, in pursuit of the purposes stated in Article 1, act in accordance with the following principles:

1. All Members and Nodes are equal in standing; no Manuscript is weighed by the institutional pedigree, wealth, or membership status of its author;
2. All Members are to fulfil their obligations under this Charter, the Doctrine, and the OSJ Rules in good faith;
3. All disputes are to be resolved by peaceful means, in a manner consistent with the Doctrine's [Rules of Interpretation](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#3-rules-of-interpretation);
4. Nothing in this Charter authorises The Open Source Journal to impose Gatekeeping, nor to intervene in matters which are the sovereign concern of a Nation-State, save as the Doctrine expressly provides.

### Article 3

The definitions set out in [Section 2](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) of the Doctrine, including "Manuscript," "Node," "Gatekeeping," "The Soul," and "The Body," apply equally to this Charter. Where this Charter uses a defined term, it carries the meaning the Doctrine assigns to it, and this Charter creates no separate or narrower meaning of its own.

## CHAPTER II: MEMBERSHIP (ARTICLES 4-9)

### Article 4

Membership of The Open Source Journal is open to every person, organisation, or Node that adopts this Charter and the Doctrine and undertakes to carry out the obligations they contain.

### Article 5

1. Admission to Membership requires no fee, credential, sponsorship, or institutional affiliation.
2. Admission is effected by adoption in fact: any Node that submits, mirrors, or transmits a Manuscript under the Doctrine is thereby a Member for so long as it continues to do so in good faith.

### Article 6

A Member may withdraw from The Open Source Journal at any time, by ceasing to submit, mirror, or transmit any Manuscript under the Doctrine. Withdrawal takes effect immediately, does not require the consent of any organ, and does not affect any Manuscript the Member has already committed to the network, nor any right the Doctrine grants to third parties in respect of it.

### Article 7

A Member against which enforcement action has been taken under Chapter VII of this Charter may have the exercise of the rights and privileges of Membership suspended by the OSJ Sovereign Committee, upon the consensus of the OSJ Members, until Gatekeeping has ceased.

### Article 8

A Member which persistently violates the principles of Article 2, in particular by imposing Gatekeeping upon Manuscripts or other Members, may be expelled from Membership by the OSJ Sovereign Committee upon the consensus of the OSJ Members. Expulsion removes standing within The Open Source Journal; it does not, and cannot, remove a Manuscript already committed to the network, nor alter any right the Doctrine grants to third parties.

### Article 9

A Member suspended under Article 7 or expelled under Article 8 may apply to the OSJ Sovereign Committee for reinstatement upon the cessation of the Gatekeeping found against it. Reinstatement is granted upon the consensus of the OSJ Members, and may not be withheld on any ground amounting to Gatekeeping under [Section 2(f)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) of the Doctrine.

## CHAPTER III: ORGANS (ARTICLES 10-11)

### Article 10

1. There are established as the principal organs of The Open Source Journal: a Consensus of Members (the primary deliberative organ), the OSJ Sovereign Committee (the executive organ), the OSJ Sovereign National Committees (regional organs), a Dispute Resolution process (the tribunal-equivalent organ), and a Registrar (the administrative organ).
2. Such subsidiary organs as may be found necessary may be established in accordance with the OSJ Rules, strictly within the limits the Soul imposes.

### Article 11

No Member shall be denied equal participation in the organs of The Open Source Journal by reason of the eligibility prerequisites, financial capacity, or institutional affiliation that [Section 2(f)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) of the Doctrine forbids.

## CHAPTER IV: THE CONSENSUS OF MEMBERS (ARTICLES 12-16)

### Article 12

The Consensus of Members consists of all Members of The Open Source Journal in good standing.

### Article 13

The Consensus of Members is the body by which disputes are resolved under [Section 14](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#14-dispute-resolution) of the Doctrine, by which the Chairman's succession is directed under [Section 9(4)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#9-appointment-and-dilution-of-the-chair), and by which amendments to the Body are adopted under [Section 5(2)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#5-absolute-immutability-of-the-soul).

### Article 14

Each Member has one voice in the Consensus of Members. No weight is given on account of wealth, institutional standing, or the size of any Node's contribution.

### Article 15

A matter is settled by consensus when it has been published for open comment for the period the OSJ Rules fix, and no Member maintains a reasoned objection at the close of that period. Where a reasoned objection is maintained, the matter is resolved by such further process as the OSJ Rules provide, save that no such process may itself take the form of Gatekeeping, or weigh a Member's voice by wealth, institutional standing, or the size of its contribution, contrary to Article 14.

### Article 16

The Open Source Journal maintains no treasury and levies no fee upon any Member. Its operation depends upon the voluntary contribution of Nodes, consistent with [Section 12](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#12-prohibition-of-institutional-barriers) of the Doctrine, which forbids financial exclusion as a condition of participation.

## CHAPTER V: THE OSJ SOVEREIGN COMMITTEE (ARTICLES 17-20)

### Article 17

The OSJ Sovereign Committee is composed as [Section 9(1)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#9-appointment-and-dilution-of-the-chair) of the Doctrine provides: the OSJ Chairman, the President (Co-Chairman), the Vice President, and the Executive Members of the States.

### Article 18

The OSJ Sovereign Committee holds final authority on all proposed OSJ Rules, as [Section 8(3)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#8-establishment-of-the-committees) of the Doctrine provides, and administers the day-to-day governance of The Open Source Journal between sittings of the Consensus of Members.

### Article 19

The seat of the OSJ Chairman is subject to permanent dilution upon the vacation of office of its founding Chairman, Ratio Juris, exactly as [Section 9(3)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#9-appointment-and-dilution-of-the-chair) of the Doctrine provides. No provision of this Charter may be read to create a route around that dilution.

### Article 20

The President, the Vice President, or an Executive Member of the States, other than the Chairman, may be removed for cause by the Consensus of Members, upon a finding that the officer concerned has persistently violated Article 2. No removal under this Article may be used to achieve, in effect, an appointment to the Chairman's seat that Article 19 forbids.

## CHAPTER VI: PACIFIC SETTLEMENT OF DISPUTES (ARTICLES 21-22)

### Article 21

Members shall first seek a solution to any dispute by negotiation, mediation, or open public scrutiny of the Manuscript or conduct concerned, before resort to Article 22.

### Article 22

Where negotiation under Article 21 fails, the dispute is referred to the Consensus of Members for resolution under [Section 14](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#14-dispute-resolution) of the Doctrine. The decision of the Consensus of Members is final, save that it may not alter, dilute, or contradict the Soul.

## CHAPTER VII: ACTION WITH RESPECT TO GATEKEEPING (ARTICLES 23-25)

### Article 23

The OSJ Sovereign Committee shall determine the existence of any act of Gatekeeping by a Member and shall recommend measures to secure its cessation.

### Article 24

Measures available under this Chapter include public notice of the violation, suspension under Article 7, removal of a Node's standing as an official mirror, and, in a persistent case, expulsion under Article 8. No measure under this Chapter may itself take the form of Gatekeeping.

### Article 25

Nothing in this Chapter empowers The Open Source Journal to compel any Nation-State, nor to reach into matters of domestic law beyond the terms of [Section 1(2)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#1-short-title-extent-and-commencement) of the Doctrine.

## CHAPTER VIII: REGIONAL ARRANGEMENTS (ARTICLES 26-27)

### Article 26

Nothing in this Charter precludes the existence of OSJ Sovereign National Committees, or other regional arrangements, for dealing with matters of copyright and intellectual property enforcement as are appropriate for regional action, provided their activity is consistent with the purposes and principles of Chapter I.

### Article 27

An OSJ Sovereign National Committee acts subject to the final authority of the OSJ Sovereign Committee under [Section 8(3)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#8-establishment-of-the-committees) of the Doctrine, and may be dissolved by it upon a finding that the Committee concerned has acted inconsistently with Chapter I.

## CHAPTER IX: INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION (ARTICLE 28)

### Article 28

1. The Open Source Journal shall promote cooperation with co-operating Nation-States, academic institutions willing to abolish Gatekeeping, and technological platforms willing to remove purchased-certificate barriers to trust,<sup id="cfnref2"><a class="fnref" href="#cfn2">[2]</a></sup> in furtherance of the purposes stated in Article 1.
2. Cooperation under this Article is voluntary on the part of the Nation-State, institution, or platform concerned, and creates no obligation binding upon it beyond what it has itself accepted, consistent with [Section 1(2)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#1-short-title-extent-and-commencement) of the Doctrine.

## CHAPTER X: DISPUTE RESOLUTION (THE TRIBUNAL-EQUIVALENT ORGAN) (ARTICLES 29-30)

### Article 29

Jurisprudence under this Charter is drawn from the concepts of Judicial Justice and Absolute Justice as defined in [Section 2(g)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) of the Doctrine, and, where published, as further elaborated in the *[Doctrine of Judicial Justice and Absolute Justice](./JJAJ.md)*.

### Article 30

Every Member undertakes to comply with a decision of the Consensus of Members reached under Article 22, in any dispute to which it is a party.

## CHAPTER XI: THE REGISTRAR (ARTICLES 31-32)

### Article 31

1. There is established a Registrar, responsible for maintaining the authoritative text of the Doctrine, this Charter, the OSJ License, the OSJ Rules, and the citation record supporting them.
2. The Registrar is appointed by the OSJ Sovereign Committee, and may be removed for cause by the Consensus of Members.

### Article 32

The Registrar acts independently of any single Node, publisher, or Nation-State, and shall not itself impose any eligibility prerequisite, fee, or credential upon any Member as a condition of maintaining the record.

## CHAPTER XII: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS (ARTICLES 33-35)

### Article 33

This Charter, once adopted, is to be made available in full alongside the Doctrine, without restriction, fee, or condition of access.

### Article 34

In the event of any conflict between the provisions of this Charter and the Doctrine, the Doctrine prevails, and this Charter is to be read subject to it, consistent with [Section 2(i)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#2-interpretation-and-definitions) and [Section 4(4)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#4-the-soul-and-the-body) of the Doctrine.

### Article 35

Where this Charter is translated, the English text remains authoritative unless the Consensus of Members determines otherwise. A translation is a derivative for the purposes of the Doctrine and The OSJ License, and must carry attribution accordingly.

## CHAPTER XIII: RATIFICATION AND AMENDMENT (ARTICLES 36-38)

### Article 36

This Charter enters into force upon its publication alongside the Doctrine, and requires no separate ratification by any Member, Node, or Nation-State beyond the act of adoption described in Article 4.

### Article 37

This Charter may be amended by the process the Doctrine's [Section 5(2)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#5-absolute-immutability-of-the-soul) and [Section 6(3)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#6-the-regulator-of-the-os-journal) provide for the Body. No amendment may alter, dilute, or contradict the Soul, and no amendment may achieve, in the form of a Charter revision, an outcome [Section 5](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#5-absolute-immutability-of-the-soul) or [Section 10(2)](./OSJ_DOCTRINE.md#10-internal-law) of the Doctrine would otherwise forbid.

### Article 38

No amendment adopted under Article 37 affects a Manuscript, a Membership, or a right already vested under this Charter or the Doctrine before that amendment's adoption.

---

*End of the Charter of The Open Source Journal.*
